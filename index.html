About me

Reuven Rivlin is an Israeli politician who served as the 10th President of Israel from 2014 to 2021. He was born in Jerusalem in 1939 to a family with deep roots in the city dating back to the early 19th century. Rivlin comes from a prominent religious Zionist family and is a descendant of students of the Vilna Gaon, a revered Jewish scholar.

Before his presidency, Rivlin had a long and distinguished career in Israeli politics. He was a member of the Likud Party and served in various government positions, including as Minister of Communications, Minister of Transportation, and Speaker of the Knesset (Israeli parliament). He was known for his conservative views and advocacy for a unified Jerusalem as the capital of Israel.

Rivlin's presidency was marked by his efforts to bridge political divides within Israeli society and promote unity. He was seen as a unifying figure who sought to represent all segments of Israeli society, including Arab Israelis and religious minorities. During his presidency, he focused on issues such as civil rights, social justice, and the relationship between Israel and the Jewish diaspora.

After completing his term as President in 2021, Rivlin continued to be active in public life, advocating for issues such as democracy, human rights, and the importance of dialogue and cooperation among different communities in Israel.
